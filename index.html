SITUATIONAL POVERTY

Situational poverty is generally caused by a sudden crisis or loss and is often temporary. Events causing situational poverty include environmental disasters, divorce, or severe health problems. Generational poverty occurs in families where at least two generations have been born into poverty.

Relative poverty

Relative poverty is the level of poverty that changes based on context– it is relative to the economic climate. Relative Poverty is when a household receives 60% of the average household income in their own economy. They do have some money, however, not enough to afford anything above the basics.

Absolute poverty

Absolute poverty refers to when a person or household does not have the minimum amount of income needed to meet the minimum living requirements needed over an extended period of time. In other words, they cannot meet their basic needs. When an individual goes below this threshold their survival is threatened.
